Section 01
Problem Statement

Jar installs at scale — but the majority of users never set up a single Autopay mandate. Of those who do, a significant cohort saves for 1–2 months and silently stops. The result: Jar has 20M+ registered users but only a fraction are active daily savers contributing to AUM. The business opportunity is not more installs — it's converting and retaining the users already in the funnel.

Business context
  • Jar's revenue model depends on AUM (gold spread + platform fees). More active savers = higher AUM = higher revenue.
  • Became profitable Jan 2025 — but sustainable growth requires improving LTV per user, not just adding installs.
  • Every unactivated user is a sunk CAC cost. Activating even 10% more of existing installs is more capital-efficient than acquiring new ones.
  • Nek jewelry brand (₹100Cr+ revenue) creates a natural high-intent redemption hook that's underutilised in the savings flow.
User behavior context (Mass India)
  • Target user: 22–35 yr, Tier 2/3, monthly income ₹15K–40K. First-time digital saver.
  • Mental model: "I'll start saving when I have enough money" — deferral is the default.
  • Trust barrier: UPI Autopay = "auto-deduction from bank" — feels risky to low-income users.
  • Gold saves the day: cultural familiarity with gold removes asset-class friction, but product friction remains high.
  • External triggers (festivals, salary day) drive spikes — but no sustained daily habit is formed.
The core problem in one line

Jar has a habit formation problem, not a discovery problem. Users understand the concept but don't complete the activation step — and those who do churn within 30 days before the saving habit is cemented.

The #1 drop-off to fix first

First purchase → Autopay setup. This is where Jar's core value proposition — daily compounding gold savings — never gets experienced. A user who manually buys gold once and leaves is worth ₹X. A user with Autopay running for 6 months is worth ₹20X. Every product and growth effort should optimise this transition first.

Why D7–D30 churn is underrated

Most teams focus on activation. But D7–D30 churn destroys LTV silently. These users already set up Autopay — they crossed the hardest barrier. Losing them is pure retention failure, not acquisition failure. A 5-point improvement in D30 retention has 3–4x more LTV impact than an equivalent improvement in activation rate.

Section 04
Why Users Are Not Converting — 5 Hypotheses
H1 UPI Autopay mandate feels financially threatening to low-income users

Target users earn ₹15K–40K/month and live paycheck-to-paycheck. "UPI Autopay" reads as "bank account access" — similar to EMI deductions that have caused negative experiences. The ₹50/day default might sound small, but after salary, it triggers anxiety. Jar's UI doesn't sufficiently de-risk the mandate step with trust signals, cancellation clarity, or a "you're in control" reassurance.

Support tickets: "how do I cancel autopay" High drop on mandate confirmation screen Low autopay % in Tier 3 vs Tier 1
H2 There is no compelling "aha moment" before Autopay is asked

Users are asked to commit to daily saving before they've experienced any value. The product skips the reward loop: Install → KYC → "Set Autopay" — no dopamine hit in between. Compare to games that give you a win before asking for a purchase. Jar should show users their gold growing for 1–2 days before asking for Autopay commitment — earned commitment converts better than cold commitment.

Users who do 1 manual save before autopay = higher conversion Time between install and autopay setup is too compressed
H3 Saving amount defaults feel either too high or too abstract

₹50/day = ₹1,500/month. For a user earning ₹18K, that's 8.3% of income — not trivial. But Jar shows it as "₹50/day" without contextualising what it becomes. The nudge lacks goal anchoring ("₹50/day for 180 days = 1g gold coin for Diwali"). Without a visualised outcome, ₹50/day feels like a cost, not an investment.

Users who set custom amounts vs default Autopay conversion by amount bracket Goal-setting users vs non-goal users retention delta
H4 No habit cue or external trigger fires during the critical D1–D7 window

Habit formation requires cue → routine → reward (Duhigg loop). Jar has the routine (autopay) and a weak reward (balance ticks up) but no strong cue. There are no personalized nudges tied to salary credit day, no streak notifications, no "you saved for 3 days in a row" moment. D1–D7 is quiet after setup — and silence = forgetting.

Push notification CTR D1-D7 is low App opens drop sharply post autopay setup Mandate failure rate spikes on non-salary days
H5 Autopay pause/failure is a permanent exit — no recovery flow exists

One failed mandate (due to low balance) likely pauses Autopay without a smart recovery nudge. For mass India users, month-end is tight. A failed deduction sends no empathetic re-engagement — just silence or a generic error. Users who pause once rarely return. A recovery flow with "resume saving" + showing what they missed could salvage 20–30% of lapsed savers.

Section 05
5 Growth Experiments (A/B Tests)
01
Goal-First Onboarding ("Save for What?")
+8–12% Autopay activation
Hypothesis

Users who anchor saving to a named goal (wedding, Diwali, child's education) before Autopay setup will activate at a higher rate — because commitment has personal meaning, not just financial logic.

Setup

Control: Current flow — KYC → amount → Autopay setup.

Variant: Insert a goal-selection screen post-KYC: "What are you saving for?" (icons: Wedding 💍, Diwali, Education, Emergency, Other). Auto-calculate: "Save ₹X/day for Y months to reach your goal." Then Autopay setup. Split: 50/50. Sample: 50K new users/cohort.

Expected Outcome

Goal-first variant shows +8–12% lift in Autopay setup rate. Secondary: goal-setting users show 15–20% better D30 retention (goal = anchor against churn). Monitor: goal abandonment rate to ensure screen doesn't add drop-off.

Metrics: autopay_setup_rate goal_screen_completion_rate D30_retention_by_goal_type mandate_amount_set
02
Trust Reframe — "You're Always in Control" Autopay Screen
+6–10% mandate completion
Hypothesis

Explicitly addressing the "I'm giving bank access" fear on the Autopay screen — with clear cancellation copy, a "pause anytime" assurance, and a lock-icon UI — will reduce psychological barrier and lift mandate completion.

Setup

Control: Current Autopay screen with standard UPI mandate UI.

Variant A: Add 3 trust bullets below the CTA: "✓ Pause anytime, no penalty" / "✓ ₹50/day max — you set the limit" / "✓ 12 lakh+ families saving daily." Variant B: Same + add a FAQ toggle "How does Autopay work?" inline. 3-way split, 30K users each.

Expected Outcome

Variant A: +6–8% mandate completion. Variant B: +4–6% (FAQ adds friction). If social proof line ("12 lakh families") drives uplift, roll out across all onboarding screens. Winner becomes default within 3 weeks.

Metrics: mandate_screen_CTR mandate_completion_rate FAQ_toggle_open_rate post_mandate_cancel_rate_D3
03
Salary Day Trigger — Smart Autopay Timing
+15–20% D30 retention
Hypothesis

Mandate failures occur most on non-salary days. Users who set Autopay aligned to their salary credit date will have fewer failed mandates and higher D30 retention than those on a fixed daily deduction regardless of balance.

Setup

Control: Autopay defaults to ₹50/day, next day start.

Variant: Ask "When do you get your salary?" (1st/5th/10th/15th of month or weekly). Adjust Autopay to trigger 1 day after salary. Also send a "Salary came in! Your gold saved today 🪙" push on that day. 50/50 split, 40K users.

Expected Outcome

Mandate failure rate drops 30–40%. D30 retention improves 15–20% in salary-aligned cohort. Increased perceived reliability of the product ("it only deducts when I have money"). Bonus: salary-day push notification becomes highest-open-rate touchpoint.

Metrics: mandate_failure_rate D30_retention salary_day_push_open_rate avg_saving_days_per_month
04
Saving Streak + Loss Aversion Notification
+10–15% D7 → D30 retention
Hypothesis

Users with a visible streak counter and loss-aversion nudges ("Your 6-day saving streak ends tonight!") will open the app more frequently and maintain their Autopay longer than users with no streak mechanic, because loss aversion > reward motivation for mass India users.

Setup

Control: No streak. Current generic push notifications.

Variant A: Streak counter on home screen. D6 evening push: "Don't break your 6-day streak! 🔥 Save today." Variant B: Streak + quantified loss: "You'll lose 0.02g gold momentum if you pause today." 3-way split. Run for 60 days, minimum 25K per arm.

Expected Outcome

Variant A: +10% D30 retention vs control. Variant B: +15% (quantified gold loss more motivating). App open rate D5–D7 increases by 20%+ in streak variants. Monitor: streak anxiety — ensure it doesn't cause negative sentiment (track uninstall rate).

Metrics: streak_notification_open_rate D7_D30_retention_delta app_opens_D5-D7 uninstall_rate_by_variant
05
Lapsed Saver Re-engagement — "Your Gold Missed You" Flow
+20–25% lapsed user reactivation
Hypothesis

Users who experience a mandate failure or pause and receive an empathetic, goal-anchored re-engagement sequence (not a generic "resume saving" push) within 24 hours will reactivate at significantly higher rates than those receiving no or generic nudge.

Setup

Trigger: mandate_failed OR autopay_paused event.
Control: No action / current generic push.
Variant: 3-step sequence: (T+2h) WhatsApp: "Your Diwali gold goal paused. Resume with ₹1 less/day?" → (T+24h) Push: "You were 12 days into your streak." → (T+72h) SMS: Gold price update + "Prices up 2% this week — resume now?" Split: 50/50, 20K lapsed users.

Expected Outcome

Variant: 20–25% of lapsed users restart Autopay within 7 days vs ~8% control. WhatsApp step has highest conversion (higher open rate than push). Gold price trigger in SMS is best timed re-entry hook. Revenue impact: recovering 15K lapsed users/month at ₹50/day avg = ₹22.5L additional AUM/month.

Metrics: reactivation_rate_D7 channel_conversion_by_step AUM_recovered re-churn_rate_after_reactivation

Prioritization logic
  • Week 1–2: Ship E2 (trust reframe) — pure copy change, no engineering, immediate mandate lift.
  • Week 3–5: Launch E1 (goal-first) — 1 new screen, highest activation impact.
  • Week 4–6: Launch E4 (streak) — push notification logic, moderate eng lift.
  • Week 5–7: Launch E3 (salary timing) — UPI date logic, slightly more complex.
  • Week 6–8: Launch E5 (re-engagement) — WhatsApp + push sequence, highest async complexity.
Key assumptions & risks
  • Baseline metrics are estimates — actual numbers may shift experiments' priority.
  • E3 depends on accurate salary-day capture — needs validation (bank statement API or self-declared).
  • E4 streak mechanic risk: anxiety → uninstall if poorly calibrated. Must monitor D7 uninstall rate closely.
  • E5 WhatsApp channel requires Jar's WABA approval and template pre-approval — lead time 2–3 weeks.
  • All experiments need minimum 2-week run time and 95% statistical significance before calling winner.

The underlying insight across all 5 experiments: Jar's problem is not awareness — it's the gap between intent and habit. Every experiment is designed to shrink that gap by reducing friction, building trust, adding goal anchors, and catching users before they slip away permanently.
